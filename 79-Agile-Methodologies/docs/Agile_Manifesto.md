# Agile Methodologies Manifesto: Adaptive Engineering Frameworks (ADD-79)

## 1. Executive Summary
Agile is not a single process, but a mindset for delivering value in volatile environments. We leverage specific frameworks to balance speed, quality, and coordination across teams, ensuring that our architectural and product decisions remain responsive to stakeholder feedback.

## 2. Core Frameworks
- **Scrum:** Iterative delivery focused on time-boxed Sprints, clear roles (PO, SM, Team), and empirical process control.
- **Kanban:** Continuous flow management visualizing work-in-progress (WIP) to optimize throughput and eliminate bottlenecks.
- **XP (Extreme Programming):** Engineering excellence via Pair Programming, TDD (Test-Driven Development), and Continuous Integration.
- **SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework):** Scaling Agile principles to large enterprise organizations (Portfolio/Program/Team levels).
- **LeSS (Large-Scale Scrum):** Simplifying organizational complexity by scaling Scrum without adding bureaucratic overhead.

## 3. Engineering Philosophy
- **Velocity:** Measuring sustainable pace rather than raw output.
- **Empiricism:** Inspecting and adapting via transparent workflows.
- **Flow Efficiency:** Reducing context switching by limiting concurrent work items.
