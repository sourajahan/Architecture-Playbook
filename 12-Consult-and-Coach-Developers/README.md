# 12. Consult & Coach Developers: Scaling Through People

## Philosophy: Mentorship as Architectural Scalability
A senior architect who acts as a single point of failure is a design flaw. My primary objective is to make myself redundant by cultivating architectural thinking across the engineering team. If the team cannot make high-quality design decisions without my intervention, I have failed as a coach.

## Strategic Approach

### 1. Design Reviews as Education
- **Empowerment over Correction:** Reviewing code or architecture is not about "fixing" the work; it is about explaining the *why* behind design principles (e.g., SOLID, separation of concerns, scalability).
- **Socratic Method:** I prefer asking guiding questions rather than giving direct orders. *"What trade-offs did you consider here?"* or *"How would this component behave under high load?"* are powerful coaching tools.

### 2. Knowledge Sharing Culture
- **Tech Talks & Workshops:** I regularly facilitate sessions on new patterns, tools, or architectural decisions to align the team's technical language.
- **Living Documentation:** I maintain and encourage the use of architectural guidelines that act as a guidebook for junior engineers, reducing the need for constant supervision.

### 3. Tactful Pair Programming
- Engaging in tactical pair programming sessions on complex modules ensures architectural consistency and transfers "tribal knowledge" that documentation might miss.

## The Goal
To build a self-sustaining engineering team where high-level architectural standards are applied naturally. I measure my success by the quality of decisions the team makes when I am not in the room.
