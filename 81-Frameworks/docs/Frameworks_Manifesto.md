# Frameworks Manifesto: Enterprise Standards and Modeling (ADD-81)

## 1. Executive Summary
Standardized frameworks serve as the foundation for communication and structural alignment in large-scale enterprises. By adopting established methodologies like TOGAF, BABOK, IAF, and UML, we ensure that our architectural decisions are consistent, justifiable, and easily understood by stakeholders across the organization.

## 2. Core Framework Pillars
- **TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework):** Providing the ADM (Architecture Development Method) for managing the enterprise architecture lifecycle.
- **BABOK (Business Analysis Body of Knowledge):** Standardizing the definition of requirements, business needs, and solution validation.
- **IAF (Integrated Architecture Framework):** Enabling holistic views of business and technology integration.
- **UML (Unified Modeling Language):** Using standardized notation to visualize and communicate system design.

## 3. Implementation Strategy
- **Layered Architecture:** Applying the TOGAF domains (Business, Data, Application, Technology) to compartmentalize complexity.
- **Modeling First:** Utilizing UML diagrams as the "Single Source of Truth" before physical implementation.
- **Traceability:** Mapping business requirements (BABOK) directly to architectural components to ensure total alignment.
